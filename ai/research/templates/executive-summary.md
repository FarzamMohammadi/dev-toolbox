# Executive Summary Template

> A standalone 1-page summary for busy readers who need the key takeaways.

---

```markdown
# Executive Summary: [TOPIC]

**Research Date**: [Date] | **Depth**: [Level] | **Confidence**: [Overall %]

---

## The Question

[1-2 sentences restating what was researched in clear terms]

---

## Key Findings

1. **[Most important finding]** (HIGH confidence)
   [1 sentence elaboration]

2. **[Second finding]** (HIGH confidence)
   [1 sentence elaboration]

3. **[Third finding]** (MEDIUM confidence)
   [1 sentence elaboration]

4. **[Fourth finding]** (MEDIUM confidence)
   [1 sentence elaboration]

---

## The Bottom Line

[2-3 sentence synthesis - what does this all mean? What should the reader
take away? What's the actionable insight?]

---

## Important Caveats

- **[Caveat 1]**: [Brief description of when findings don't apply]
- **[Caveat 2]**: [Important limitation to know]
- **[Caveat 3]**: [Edge case or exception]

---

## Confidence Assessment

| Area | Confidence | Note |
|------|------------|------|
| [Finding 1] | HIGH | Strong multi-source support |
| [Finding 2] | HIGH | Academic + practical consensus |
| [Finding 3] | MEDIUM | Good support, some caveats |
| [Finding 4] | MEDIUM | Limited Tier 1-2 sources |

**Overall Research Confidence**: [X]%

---

- Complete analysis of [N] sub-questions

- Source cards and methodology

---

*[N] sources analyzed | [N] searches conducted | [Duration]*
```

---

## Guidelines

### Length
- **Maximum**: 1 page when printed/viewed
- **Target**: ~300-400 words
- **Minimum**: Cover all sections meaningfully

### Tone
- Direct and confident (but honest about uncertainty)
- Active voice
- No hedging beyond what's necessary
- Business-appropriate

### What to Include
- The single most important thing to know (lead with it)
- Top 4-5 findings only
- Critical caveats (not all caveats)
- Clear confidence levels
- Pointer to full report

### What to Exclude
- Methodology details
- Full source citations
- Detailed counter-evidence
- Edge cases unless critical
- Background/context (reader knows why they asked)
